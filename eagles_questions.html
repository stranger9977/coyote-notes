Interview day · reverse interview

Questions for them

Good questions show you're already thinking like you have the job. Listen as hard as you ask.

My openers

  1. Who's your favorite pick from this year's draft class, and why? Are there any Day 3 picks you're especially excited about — guys who could make an outsized impact? Signals you've studied the class; their answer tells you how the building actually values traits vs. production, and how Day-3/UDFA bets get made.
  2. What's your favorite under-the-radar free-agent signing this offseason — a low-cost add you think could outperform the price tag? Pairs with the draft question and is very Eagles — value-hunting at the bottom of the roster. Surfaces how they think about cheap upside (the Ebiketie / Pierce / Moore-type bets).
  3. What's the most challenging part of the job? Invites candor and shows you want the real picture, not the brochure. Listen for whether it's technical, organizational, or buy-in.
  4. What's your favorite part of the job? Warm, human, and tells you what energizes the team you'd join.

The role & the day-to-day

  1. What does a typical week look like in-season versus the offseason for this role? Sets expectations and shows you're thinking about fit, not just the title.
  2. What does success look like in the first 6–12 months — how will you know I'm doing well? One of the strongest questions you can ask; gives you a concrete target and shows ownership.
  3. How's the time split between long-horizon research projects and quick, same-day questions from coaches or the front office? Reveals whether it's a deep-work shop or a fire-drill service desk.
  4. What does the analytics stack look like today — languages, the tracking-data pipeline, modeling tools — and where are the biggest gaps you'd want help closing? Shows you'll be useful Day 1 and surfaces where you can add value fast.

How analytics drives decisions

  1. Can you walk me through a recent decision where analytics genuinely changed the outcome — and maybe one where the data got overruled, and why? The single most revealing question about how much the work actually matters here.
  2. How does analytics reach the people making the calls — is the group embedded with coaches and scouts, or more of a central service? Tells you how your work travels and whether you'll see it land.
  3. Where do you see the biggest untapped edge right now — tracking data, special teams, in-game decisions, draft/roster modeling? Shows strategic thinking and teases out where they want to grow (and where you'd fit).
  4. How do you measure the impact of the analytics group itself? Sharp, slightly meta — shows you care about outcomes, not just outputs.

Collaboration & culture

  1. How do analysts, coaches, and scouts actually work together — and how does someone earn buy-in from football people who are skeptical of numbers? Communicating to non-technical decision-makers is the whole game; asking this shows you get that.
  2. When "the model says X" collides with football intuition, how does the team handle it? Reveals the culture's maturity around analytics — and your own humility.
  3. How would you describe the culture of the group and the building? Simple, honest, and the answer's tone tells you a lot.

Growth

  1. How has this role evolved over the last few years, and where do you see it heading?
  2. What separates the analysts who thrive here from the ones who struggle? Gets you the unwritten success criteria.

Show you know ball (use 1–2, don't force it)

  1. How heavily is player tracking / Next Gen Stats used today, and is there appetite to build counterfactual, "ghosting"-style player-evaluation metrics in-house? Ties straight to your COYOTE prep — signals you're already operating in their problem space.
  2. Special teams is famously under-measured — how much analytics attention does it get, and is there room to push there? A pointed, on-brand question (it's literally what COYOTE tackles).
  3. How do you think about build-vs-buy between third-party models (PFF, Sumer, Next Gen) and proprietary in-house work? Shows you understand the practical economics of an analytics shop.

Closers

  1. Is there anything about my background that gives you pause that I could speak to right now? Bold, and it lets you address doubts before you leave the room.
  2. What are the next steps — and is there anything I can send over that would be helpful?
If you only have time for a few: the draft-pick question (you'll have it from the Offseason tab), "what does success look like in 6–12 months," "a decision analytics changed — and one where it got overruled," and "how do you earn buy-in from skeptical football people." Then close with the "anything that gives you pause" question.
How to use this: don't read a list at them — pick 3–4, and let their answers pull you into follow-ups. The best version of this is a conversation where you react to what they say. Private notes · not indexed.
